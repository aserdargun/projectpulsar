## Knowledge navigation

Technical documents, procedures, standards and records can become easier to search, compare and explain. Responses should preserve citations and source authority, with access inherited from the systems that own the knowledge.

**Useful measures:** answer relevance, citation accuracy, time to trusted information and escalation rate.

## Operational intelligence

Time-series signals, event histories and operating context can be summarized into anomalies, hypotheses and structured investigation paths. Calculations remain in deterministic tools; the model supports interpretation rather than inventing measurements.

**Useful measures:** investigation time, hypothesis usefulness, false confidence and analyst acceptance.

## Engineering and maintenance support

Structured records and unstructured reports can be connected to reveal recurring patterns, similar cases and missing evidence. Critical recommendations remain visible as decision support and require appropriate review.

**Useful measures:** evidence coverage, review time, repeatability and safe rejection behavior.

## Software and analytical work

Bounded assistants can help explain code, draft tests, compare changes and prepare analyses. Repository permissions, tool access, execution limits and human approval protect the delivery path.

**Useful measures:** review acceptance, defect escape rate, cycle time and tool-policy compliance.

## Decision briefs

PULSAR can assemble source-linked summaries, competing interpretations and open questions for human decision-makers. It should make uncertainty easier to see—not hide it behind fluent text.

**Useful measures:** traceability, omitted-risk rate, time to decision and reviewer confidence.

## A disciplined starting rule

A use case is ready for validation when it has a clear owner, a permitted dataset, a repeatable test set, measurable success criteria and a safe way to decline or escalate. If those conditions are missing, the next step is discovery—not deployment.
